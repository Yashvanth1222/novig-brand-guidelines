08 — Brand Tone

Brand Tone Guide

How Novig sounds across every touchpoint. These five principles define our voice — use them as a guide for any public-facing communication.

1
Edgy

We're not afraid to say what everyone in sports betting already knows but won't say out loud: the house has been rigging the game. Novig calls it out — directly, confidently, and without pulling punches. Our tone has an edge because we're building something that genuinely disrupts a broken system.

✓ Do
Challenge the status quo. Take a stance. Name the problem plainly.
✗ Don't
Be mean-spirited, punch down, or be edgy for its own sake. The edge comes from truth, not attitude.
2
Welcoming

Sharp doesn't mean cold. We want every bettor — from the seasoned sharp to someone placing their first bet — to feel like Novig is on their side. The tone is warm and inclusive, never gatekeeping or condescending.

✓ Do
Invite people in. Make them feel smart for choosing Novig. Explain, don't lecture.
✗ Don't
Assume knowledge, use jargon without context, or make casual bettors feel like outsiders.
3
Make the Complex Accessible

The exchange model, vig, juice, market efficiency — these concepts are genuinely important and genuinely confusing. Our job is to make them feel obvious, not overwhelming. We don't dumb things down; we light them up.

✓ Do
Use analogies, plain language, and concrete examples. Trust readers to follow along when you lead clearly.
✗ Don't
Oversimplify to the point of losing accuracy, or overcomplicate to the point of losing people.
4
The Smarter Alternative

Novig earns its place not by shouting louder, but by being right. The tone is confident and grounded — we let the math and the model do the talking. We're the choice for people who've done the homework.

✓ Do
Lead with evidence. Be specific about advantages (lower fees, better prices, transparency). Let the product speak.
✗ Don't
Over-claim, rely on hype, or make vague "better" statements without backing them up.
5
Open Doors

Sports trading is for everyone. If you care about the game and want a better way to bet on it, you're exactly who we're building for. We don't care where you're from, how long you've been doing this, or what your bankroll looks like — if you want in, you're in. Our tone reflects that openness: no velvet rope, no insider handshake, just a better product that works for anyone willing to use it.

✓ Do
Write like you're talking to anyone and everyone. Make people feel like they arrived at the right place.
✗ Don't
Signal exclusivity, assume a particular kind of fan or bettor, or make the brand feel like a club with membership requirements.
